Sambit Panda is an AI Research Scientist at the MATRIX AI Consortium. His primary focus is on extending AI algorithms to build assistive tools in health care. Currently, he is working on a project that aims to improve outcomes for trauma patients. He received a PhD and MSE from Johns Hopkins University, where he was advised by Dr. Joshua T. Vogelstein, and a North Carolina State University in 2018, where he worked under Dr. Leslie Sombers. He has also previously done research in two labs at the National Institutes of Environmental Health Sciences and Burleson Research Technologies. He received the Goodnight Scholarship in 2018 and the National Institutes of Health T32-a Grant in 2020. He has also reviewed papers for SoftwareX and SciPy (2020, 2021) and chaired for SciPy (2020, 2021, 2023).
